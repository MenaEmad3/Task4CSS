The Great Pyramid

  • Built around 2600 BC for Pharaoh Khufu.
  • Largest and oldest of the Giza pyramids.
  • Was the tallest human-made structure for over 3,800 years.

Contains three main chambers :

  1. King's Chamber.
  2. Queen's Chamber.
  3. Subterranean Chamber.
Originally 146.6 meters tall.
Now 138.5 meters.
Made of 2.3 million limestone blocks.
Each weighing about 2.5 tonnes.
Some blocks weigh up to 80 tonnes.

One of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, stands as a testament to the architectural brilliance and ambition of ancient Egypt . Built over 4,500 years ago during the reign of Pharaoh Khufu,

This colossal structure originally rose to a height of 146.6 meters, though erosion has slightly reduced it over time.
Composed of millions of precisely cut limestone blocks, the pyramid was constructed with astounding precision, aligning perfectly with cardinal directions.
Historians and engineers continue to marvel at how ancient builders managed such a feat without modern machinery.
Serving as a royal tomb, the Great Pyramid symbolizes the Egyptians' deep spiritual beliefs, particularly their quest for immortality.

Today, it remains an iconic landmark.
